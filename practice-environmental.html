Common questions
What is the Ley de Costas and why does it matter for my property? +

The Ley de Costas protects Spain's public coastal domain and restricts building near the shoreline. Some existing properties sit within protected zones under old rules and carry restrictions on renovation, extension, or in rare cases demolition. Checking this before buying is essential, and we do it as part of due diligence.

Can I be forced to demolish a property near the coast? +

It happens, but usually only after a formal process involving the coastal authority and, if contested, the courts. If you have received a demolition order or notice, do not ignore it. There are often grounds to challenge it or negotiate an alternative, but the deadlines to respond are strict.

Do I need an environmental permit for my business? +

It depends on the activity and its potential impact. Certain businesses, particularly in energy, industry, tourism infrastructure, or anything near protected land, need specific environmental licences before they can legally operate. We check what applies to your specific case before you commit to a location or project.

I want to buy land to build on. What should I check first? +

Before signing anything, check the land's urban classification (whether it is buildable), any coastal or environmental restrictions, and whether the town hall's local plan allows what you intend to build. Buying land without this check is one of the most common and expensive mistakes foreign buyers make in Spain.

What happens if I am fined for an environmental breach? +

Fines vary widely depending on the breach and its severity, and can come with an order to restore the site to its original condition. We review the notice, check whether the process was followed correctly, and advise whether to pay, appeal, or negotiate.
