Common questions

Before you get in touch.

Do I need a lawyer to buy property in Spain? +

Technically no, but practically it is essential. A Spanish property transaction involves legal due diligence (checking for debts, mortgages, urban planning restrictions, community charges), drafting and reviewing the arras contract, and representing you at the notary. Without an independent lawyer reviewing the transaction on your behalf, you take on all these risks personally.

Can you handle my case if I am not in Spain? +

Yes. With a notarised power of attorney, we can represent you at every stage without you travelling. Many of our international clients have their matters, from property purchases to court cases, handled entirely remotely, with updates by email and video call.

What is an NIE number and how do I get one? +

An NIE (Número de Identificación de Extranjero) is a tax identification number required for virtually all legal and financial transactions in Spain, including property purchases, opening bank accounts and paying taxes. We can apply for it on your behalf with a power of attorney, or guide you through the process at a Spanish consulate or police station.

What residency options exist for British nationals after Brexit? +

British nationals who were resident in Spain before 31 December 2020 are protected under the Withdrawal Agreement and can apply for the TIE. Those arriving after that date must apply under standard third-country national rules, typically requiring proof of sufficient income or employment. We advise on the most appropriate route for your circumstances.

What does it cost? +

Every matter is different, so we do not publish a one-size-fits-all price list. What we do promise: you receive a clear, written fee proposal after the first assessment, before any commitment, and no surprises after that.
