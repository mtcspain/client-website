Common questions
My club has stopped paying me. What can I do? +

Unpaid wages or bonuses are usually pursued first through a formal claim to the club, and if that fails, before the relevant federation body or, in some cases, the ordinary courts. Timing matters here, since contracts and federation rules often set short deadlines to bring a claim.

What are image rights and why should they be a separate agreement? +

Image rights cover the commercial use of a player's name, likeness and reputation, separate from the employment contract for playing. Structuring this properly matters both for clarity on what a club or sponsor can use, and because image rights income is taxed differently to salary in Spain.

I have been accused of a doping violation. What happens next? +

You are entitled to a formal disciplinary process, including the right to respond to the allegation and be heard before any sanction is imposed. Getting legal representation involved early, before you respond to the federation, makes a real difference to how the case is handled.

Can a federation really stop me registering with a new club? +

Federations do have rules that can block or delay a registration, for example over a contract dispute with a previous club. If you believe a block is wrong or the federation has not followed its own procedure, this can be challenged before the appropriate sports tribunal.

Do you work with clients outside Spain? +

Yes. We regularly represent players, agents and clubs based abroad on matters that touch Spanish sport, whether that is a transfer into Spain, a dispute with a Spanish club, or a contract governed by Spanish law.
