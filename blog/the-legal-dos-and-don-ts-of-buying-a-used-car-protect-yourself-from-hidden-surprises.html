The Legal Dos and Don'ts of Buying a Used Car: Protect Yourself from Hidden Surprises

2 min readCivil Law & Disputes

Buying a used car in Spain can be a great way to save money, but it can also turn into a costly headache if you skip the legal basics. A car with unpaid fines, missing paperwork, or a hidden mechanical problem can become your problem the moment you sign. Here is how to protect yourself.

What to do

Do your homework on the specific car before you get attached to it. Ask for a vehicle history report so you know about any past accidents or repairs.

Always take it for a test drive before you commit. This is your chance to check how it actually performs, not just how it looks.

Get a mechanic you trust to inspect it before you finalise anything. A cheap inspection now can save you from an expensive surprise later.

Put the deal in writing. A proper purchase agreement should include the price, the date of the transaction, and a clear description of the car. This protects you if anything is disputed down the line.

Once you buy the car, make sure the change of ownership is registered correctly with the Directorate General of Traffic (DGT). This step protects you from being held liable for things that happen after the sale.

What to avoid

Do not buy from unauthorised sellers or through the black market. It might look like a bargain, but it can land you in legal and financial trouble.

Do not buy a car without proper documentation, meaning the circulation permit and the technical inspection card (ITV). Missing these can be a sign the car is stolen or not road legal.

Do not skip checking for unpaid taxes or fines on the car. If they are not cleared before you buy, you could end up responsible for someone else's debt.

Do not buy a car whose history you know nothing about. Hidden problems have a way of surfacing only after it is too late to do anything about them.

And do not buy without a written agreement. It is the one document that protects both your rights and your responsibilities if something goes wrong.

As a consumer in Spain, you have real legal rights when buying a used car, and it is worth knowing them before you hand over any money. A little care up front means you get to actually enjoy your new car instead of dealing with unpleasant surprises.
