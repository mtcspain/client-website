What is an advanced care directive or living will in Spain?

1 min readCivil Law & Disputes

If something happened to you and you could no longer speak for yourself, would your family know what medical care you'd actually want? For expats living in Spain, this is not a question to leave unanswered, and Spanish law gives you a clear way to put your wishes in writing.

In Spain, this document is called a "testamento vital" or "documento de voluntades anticipadas", usually translated as an advanced care directive or living will. It works much like the equivalent document in other countries, letting you set out your healthcare wishes in advance, before you are ever in a situation where you can't communicate them yourself.

Specifically, it lets you record which medical treatments you want to receive or refuse if you are no longer able to make that decision yourself. That can cover things like life-sustaining treatment, palliative care, and other end-of-life decisions.

Why it matters

Having a "testamento vital" in place means your healthcare wishes are far more likely to actually be followed. It gives clear guidance to doctors and hospital staff, and it can prevent painful disagreements between family members or between family and healthcare providers at an already difficult time.

Setting one up

To create your advanced care directive in Spain, it is best to talk to a lawyer or to the relevant regional health authority. They can walk you through the legal requirements and the exact steps to create and register the document properly.

One thing worth knowing as an expat: the rules and procedures around advanced care directives can vary from region to region in Spain. So it is worth checking with a local legal professional or the relevant regional authority to make sure your document meets the requirements where you live.
