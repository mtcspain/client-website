Canceling a Mortgage in Spain

2 min readReal Estate

In Spain, canceling a mortgage refers to the process of settling and releasing an existing mortgage on a property. This process is important as it allows the owner to eliminate any financial burden associated with the mortgage and acquire full ownership of their home or property.

There are several reasons why someone may choose to cancel a mortgage in Spain:

  1. Full debt repayment: The most common reason for canceling a mortgage is having fully paid off the mortgage debt. Once the loan has been fully repaid, the owner can request the cancellation to obtain a certificate of mortgage cancellation, which is an official document demonstrating that the debt has been fully settled.
  2. Property sale: If the decision is made to sell the property, the mortgage must be canceled to enable a clear and unencumbered transfer to the buyer. In this case, mortgage cancellation is carried out as part of the selling process, and the proceeds from the sale are used to pay off the outstanding debt before the property can be transferred.
  3. Mortgage refinancing: Occasionally, homeowners may choose to refinance their mortgage to obtain better terms or reduce monthly costs. In this case, canceling the existing mortgage is necessary to replace it with a new mortgage loan from a different bank or financial institution.

The process of mortgage cancellation in Spain generally involves the following steps:

  1. Obtaining the debt-free certificate: The owner must request a certificate from the bank or financial institution showing that the debt has been fully settled. This document is essential for the mortgage cancellation.
  2. Payment of associated costs: The owner is responsible for paying the costs associated with the mortgage cancellation, such as notary and registry fees, taxes, and administrative charges. These costs may vary, and it is important to inquire about them in advance.
  3. Cancellation deed: Once the debt-free certificate is obtained and the associated costs are paid, a notary must be consulted to execute a cancellation deed. This deed establishes the cancellation of the mortgage and proceeds to register it in the corresponding Property Registry.
  4. Registration in the Property Registry: The cancellation deed must be submitted to the Property Registry for registration and to update the registry information, removing the mortgage burden from the property.

It is important to note that the process of mortgage cancellation may vary slightly depending on the autonomous community where the property is located. Therefore, it is advisable to seek the professional advice of a lawyer specialized in real estate law, to guide and assist throughout the process.
