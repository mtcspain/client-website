Claim for improper inclusion in defaulters file

2 min readCivil Law & Disputes

Finding out you are listed in a Spanish defaulters file, such as Asnef-Equifax, Badexcug, or CIRBE, can be alarming. It can make it harder to get a loan, rent a home, or even open certain accounts. But being listed does not always mean it was done correctly, and if it was not, you have the right to do something about it.

If you believe you were added to one of these files unfairly, for example over a bank debt or other non-payment that is disputed, unclear, or simply wrong, you can take the matter to court. This is not just a technical complaint. Spanish law treats being wrongly listed as a debtor as an attack on your reputation and your fundamental rights, and it allows you to claim compensation for the damage this can cause.

What counts as "improper inclusion"

Being added to a defaulters file when the debt is genuinely valid and unpaid is normal practice. The problem arises when the listing itself was not done properly, for example the debt is not truly certain, is being disputed, or the required steps and notices were not followed. In that situation, the law treats it as an illegitimate interference with your right to your good name and reputation.

What you can claim

If we are certain that your inclusion in the file was indeed improper, you can bring a claim asking the court to award compensation for the damage caused. This falls under general Spanish civil law on responsibility for harm: someone who causes damage to another person, through fault or negligence, is obliged to repair it.

Spanish law also makes this easier for people in your position in one important way. Once it is proven that the interference with your rights was illegitimate, the existence of damage is presumed. In other words, you do not have to separately prove that you suffered harm; the law assumes it once the improper listing is established.

These cases relating to a person's honor and reputation are handled through the ordinary court procedure, regardless of the amount involved, so there is a clear path to bring the claim even if the sum in question is small.
