Seize Your Second Chance: Understanding the Second Chance Law

2 min readBankruptcy

The Second Chance Law provides individuals and freelancers with the opportunity to cancel or restructure their debts when facing insolvency. Embracing this law, debtors in financial distress can free themselves from debt burdens or establish payment mechanisms aligned with their real circumstances.

At the core of this law lies the requirement of debtor's good faith. This good faith manifests through honest behavior, both when debts were incurred and when insolvency is recognized.

What is the Second Chance Law?

The Second Chance Law is founded on the idea that, to overcome economic crises, countries like Spain must provide solutions for all citizens. The recession wreaked havoc in many nations, necessitating practical solutions not just for large corporations but for everyone. The law asserts that second chance mechanisms foster both economic growth and job preservation.

This law provides a means to mitigate the effects of Article 1911 of the Civil Code, which holds debtors accountable for all present and future assets. It enhances mechanisms for individuals to overcome insolvency, granting them a fresh start in the financial and economic system without burdensome debts.

What are the requirements to access the Second Chance Law?

To avail oneself of the Second Chance Law, debtors must meet specific legal requirements:

  1. Good Faith Debtor: Honesty and sincerity in dealing with debts and creditors are paramount.
  2. Minimum of Two Debts: Debts owed to at least two different creditors are necessary.
  3. Absence of Severe Convictions: No convictions for economic crimes, serious labor violations, or offenses against public entities in the past 10 years.
  4. Debts Incurred in Good Faith: Debts should not result from reckless or negligent behavior.
  5. Insufficient Assets: Demonstrating insolvency, indicating a lack of resources to meet obligations.

What benefits does the Second Chance Law offer?

The Second Chance Law presents two primary options:

  1. Asset Liquidation: Debtors can liquidate their assets in exchange for total debt cancellation.
  2. Payment Plan to Save Home: Debtors can protect their homes and essential assets while proposing a payment plan for a portion of the debts, with the remainder being canceled.

How to initiate the process of accessing the Second Chance Law?

Accessing the Second Chance Law involves a judicial procedure, requiring documentation to prove debts and insolvency. Legal assistance is crucial, with specialized lawyers guiding debtors through the process to ensure compliance with all necessary requirements.
