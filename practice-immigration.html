Common questions
What is the difference between the NIE and the TIE? +

The NIE (Número de Identificación de Extranjero) is a tax identification number, needed for almost everything in Spain, from opening a bank account to buying property. The TIE (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero) is the physical residency card that proves your legal status to live in Spain. You can hold an NIE without residency, but residency always comes with a TIE.

What is arraigo and could it apply to me? +

Arraigo is a route to residency for people already living in Spain, based on roots already put down here. Arraigo social requires proof of at least two years' residence and a job offer or family ties. Arraigo laboral requires proof of an existing working relationship. Arraigo familiar applies to parents of a Spanish minor or spouses of Spanish nationals. We assess which route, if any, fits your situation before filing anything.

Can I apply for a POA if I cannot be in Spain? +

Yes. A power of attorney, signed in front of a notary or at a Spanish consulate, lets us handle your application, collect documents, and complete formalities on your behalf. This is common for clients abroad who need an NIE or need to appoint us before their first visit.

My visa application was denied. Can it be appealed? +

Often, yes. Denials frequently come down to missing documentation, a technical error, or a misapplication of the criteria. We review the denial letter, identify the actual reason, and where there are grounds, file an appeal within the legal deadline, which is usually short.

How long does Spanish citizenship take? +

It depends on the route. Citizenship by residency generally requires 10 years of legal residence (2 years for nationals of Ibero-American countries, the Philippines, Andorra, Equatorial Guinea, or Portugal, and for Sephardic Jews). Processing time on top of the residency requirement can currently take a year or more, and we manage the paperwork so nothing gets held up unnecessarily.
